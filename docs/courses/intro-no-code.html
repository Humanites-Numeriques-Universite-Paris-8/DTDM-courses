Ideation around Personal Needs and Co-creation of Digital Solutions

1 Course Description

This course explores the transformative power of No-Code and Low-Code paradigms in the modern digital economy. It bridges the gap between human-centered ideation and functional digital creation. Students will move from identifying personal and organizational “pain points” to architecting, building, and co-creating sophisticated digital solutions (apps, automations, and workflows) without traditional syntax-based programming.

2 Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

2.0.1 Knowledge

  • Critically evaluate the No-Code ecosystem and its underlying visual programming logic.
  • Synthesize “User-Centric Design” principles applied to digital problem-solving.
  • Assess technical limitations and scalability of No-Code vs. traditional development.
  • Identify ethical implications and data security standards (GDPR) in cloud-native platforms.

2.0.2 Skills

  • Formulate structured ideation processes to translate needs into technical requirements.
  • Architect relational databases and complex logic workflows using visual interfaces.
  • Develop high-fidelity functional prototypes and MVPs for real-world problems.
  • Implement API integrations and automations to connect disparate digital tools.

2.0.3 Competences

  • Lead co-creation workshops between technical and non-technical stakeholders.
  • Manage the end-to-end lifecycle of a digital project from empathy mapping to deployment.
  • Demonstrate an “Agile” mindset by autonomously troubleshooting technical hurdles.

3 General Competences

The course aims to develop the following core graduate attributes: - Search, analysis and synthesis of data and information, using necessary technology. - Adaptability to new situations (Fast-moving No-Code ecosystem). - Decision-making (Architectural choices and tool selection). - Working in an interdisciplinary environment (Co-creation). - Production of free, creative and inductive thinking. - Project planning and management. - Showing social, professional and ethical responsibility.

4 Weekly Schedule (39 Contact Hours)

Total Workload: 90 Hours (39h Contact + 51h Independent).

4.1 Phase 1: Ideation & Data Architecture

4.1.1 Week 1: The No-Code Paradigm & 2026 Ecosystem

  • Objectives: Understand the shift from syntax-based to logic-based development.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Lecture (1h): History of No-Code; the “Citizen Developer” in 2026.
    • Lab (2h): Setting up the “Professional Stack.” Exploring the interfaces of Airtable, Softr, and Make.
  • Independent Study: Keep a “Pain Point Log” (Identify 3 real-world frustrations).
  • course materials

4.1.2 Week 2: Ideation & Human-Centered Design

  • Objectives: Transform personal needs into digital problem statements.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Seminar (1h): Design Thinking vs. Agile. Empathy mapping for non-technical users.
    • Workshop (2h): “Crazy 8s” sketching. Defining the MVP (Minimum Viable Product) scope.
  • Independent Study: Finalize your Project Proposal (Problem/Solution fit).
  • course materials

4.1.3 Week 3: Master Class: Data Modeling (Airtable)

  • Objectives: Build a relational database from scratch.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Lecture (1h): Relational logic: One-to-Many vs. Many-to-Many.
    • Lab (2h): Building your project’s “Source of Truth.” Linking records and creating rollups.
  • Independent Study: Populate your database with “Dummy Data” for testing.
  • course materials

4.1.4 Week 4: Advanced Backend & The API Economy (Xano)

  • Objectives: Learn server-side logic and external data communication.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Lecture (1h): Understanding REST APIs, JSON, and Webhooks.
    • Lab (2h): Building an API endpoint in Xano to fetch and filter data.
  • Independent Study: Milestone 1 Submission: Technical Schema and API Map.
  • course materials

4.2 Phase 2: Interface & Logic Integration

4.2.1 Week 5: Web Interface Design & User Portals (Softr)

  • Objectives: Create responsive web interfaces connected to dynamic data.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Lecture (1h): UI/UX Best Practices for No-Code Portals.
    • Lab (2h): Setting up conditional visibility and user permissions.
  • Independent Study: Map out your app’s navigation flow.
  • course materials

4.2.2 Week 6: Native Mobile Development (FlutterFlow)

  • Objectives: Explore native mobile functionalities (GPS, Camera, Push).
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Lecture (1h): Mobile UX constraints vs. Web Desktop.
    • Lab (2h): Drag-and-drop UI build in FlutterFlow. Connecting a data source.
  • Independent Study: Optimize one key mobile feature for your MVP.
  • course materials

4.2.3 Week 7: Workflow Automation (Make.com)

  • Objectives: Build the “Invisible Nervous System” of your application.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Lecture (1h): Automation logic: Triggers, Routers, and Error Handling.
    • Lab (2h): Building a multi-step automation (e.g., Form -> DB -> AI -> Email).
  • Independent Study: Stress-test your automation with various data inputs.
  • course materials

4.2.4 Week 8: AI-Native Apps & Agentic Workflows

  • Objectives: Integrate Large Language Models (LLMs) into No-Code apps.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Lecture (1h): Prompt Engineering and API orchestration with AI.
    • Lab (2h): Building an AI agent using Stack AI to automate content generation.
  • Independent Study: Integrate an AI feature into your final project.
  • course materials

4.3 Phase 3: Co-Creation & Final Delivery

4.3.1 Week 9: Co-Creation & Peer-Review Sprint

  • Objectives: Practice collaborative development and technical auditing.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Interactive Workshop (3h): “Pair-Building.” Students swap projects to debug and optimize each other’s logic.
  • Independent Study: Implement improvements based on peer feedback.
  • course materials

4.3.2 Week 10: User Testing & Analytical Iteration

  • Objectives: Use data to refine the User Experience.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Seminar (1h): Qualitative vs. Quantitative feedback. Using Maze and Hotjar.
    • Lab (2h): Running a live user test and documenting “friction points.”
  • Independent Study: Final UI/UX polish of the MVP.
  • course materials

4.3.3 Week 11: Governance, Security & Ethics

  • Objectives: Deploy solutions that are safe, legal, and professional.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Lecture (1h): GDPR compliance, data encryption, and vendor lock-in risks.
    • Workshop (2h): Conducting a Security Audit of your MVP.
  • Independent Study: Complete the Final Technical Dossier.
  • course materials

4.3.4 Week 12: Final Demo Day & Technical Defense

  • Objectives: Present and defend the technical architecture of your solution.
  • Contact Hours (3h):
    • Event (3h): 5-minute Pitch + 5-minute Live Demo + Q&A with Faculty.
  • Independent Study: Post-course reflection: “The Future of My Digital Solution.”
  • course materials

6 Technological Equipment Requirements

  • Hardware: Minimum 16GB RAM, modern CPU (M1/M2 or Intel i7 equivalent).
  • Connectivity: High-speed Wi-Fi 6/6E with open access to API Webhooks.
  • Software: Google Chrome, Figma, and the No-Code Stack (Airtable, Softr, Make, Xano, FlutterFlow).
