Welcome to the No-Code Revolution! 🚀

Welcome to the course. In this Master’s module, we transition from being digital consumers to being architects of digital solutions. To hit the ground running, please complete the following technical setup and your “Pre-Class Mission” before our first session.

1. Hardware & Software Requirements

As we will be running multiple resource-intensive cloud editors (Bubble, FlutterFlow, AI Agents), please ensure your machine meets these 2026 standards:

ImportantMinimum Technical Specifications
  • RAM: 16GB (Strict Minimum) for smooth multi-tab performance.
  • Browser: Google Chrome (Latest version). Most No-Code debugging extensions are optimized for Chromium.
  • Secondary Screen: Highly recommended for “Build Labs” (one screen for the instructor, one for your editor).

2. The 2026 No-Code Stack (Account Setup)

Please create accounts for the following platforms using your university (.edu) email to access educational discounts and premium features.

3. Pre-Class Mission: The “Pain Point” Log

Our course starts with Ideation. Your first task is to keep a “Frustration Journal” for the next 7 days.

AstuceYour Mission
  1. Identify 3 “Personal Needs” or manual “Pain Points” in your daily life or current workplace.
  2. Example: “I hate manually tracking my student expenses” or “Our team spends 4 hours a week copying data from emails to spreadsheets.”
  3. The Goal: Bring these 3 problems to Week 1. We will use them to define your semester-long MVP project.

5. Important Note on AI Usage

NoteThe AI Junior Developer

In this course, we treat Generative AI as your Junior Developer. You are encouraged to use LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) to help write custom logic or troubleshoot errors, provided you can explain the logic during your Oral Examination.
