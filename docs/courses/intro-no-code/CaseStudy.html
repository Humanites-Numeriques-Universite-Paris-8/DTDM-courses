Practice Case Study: The Green Campus Exchange

Building a Circular Economy Platform for Students

1. The Challenge: “The Green Campus Exchange”

Context: Your university has noticed a massive waste of resources (textbooks, electronics, and kitchen equipment) left behind by departing students. Meanwhile, incoming students struggle to afford new gear.

The Goal: You must build a functional “Circular Economy” MVP that allows students to list, borrow, or buy items within the campus community.

2. User Stories (The “Need”)

Your solution must satisfy three core user personas: 1. The Donor: Needs to quickly list an item (photo, category, condition) and choose if it’s “Free” or “For Sale.” 2. The Buyer/Borrower: Needs to browse items by category, filter by “Available” status, and request an item. 3. The Admin: Needs to validate listings before they go live to ensure they follow campus rules.

3. Technical Requirements

To pass this practice run, your architecture must include:

A. Data Architecture (Airtable)

Create a relational database with at least these tables: * Users: Names, emails, student IDs, and a “Rating” field. * Items: Linked to a User (Owner), Category, Status (Draft, Approved, Sold), and Price. * Transactions: Linked to an Item, a Buyer, and a Date.

B. The Interface (Softr)

  • A Member Area where students can see their own listings.
  • A Public Gallery with search and filter capabilities.
  • A Submission Form for new items.

C. Logic & Automation (Make.com)

  • Workflow 1: When a new item is submitted, send a notification to the Admin via email or Slack for approval.
  • Workflow 2 (AI Integration): Use an AI Agent (Stack AI or OpenAI) to automatically generate a “Marketing Description” for the item based on the student’s short title and category.

4. Success Criteria (The “Tech-Check”)

Before submitting your practice link, verify: - [ ] Can a user sign up and log in? - [ ] Does the “Add Item” form correctly populate the Airtable base? - [ ] Does the “Status” change to “Sold” automatically when a transaction is recorded? - [ ] Is the AI-generated description appearing in the item details?

5. Reflection Question (Short Answer)

In your documentation, answer the following: “If this platform grew from 100 students to 10,000, which part of your current No-Code stack would break first, and how would you fix it using a ‘Low-Code’ approach?”

AstucePro-Tip

Focus on the Data Integrity first. If your database is messy, your automations will fail!
